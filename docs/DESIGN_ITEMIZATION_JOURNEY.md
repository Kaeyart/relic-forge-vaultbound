# Design Lock — Itemization Journey

Items in Relic Forge: Vaultbound are not just stat sticks. They are rough pieces of power that the player discovers, evaluates, and sculpts.

## Core fantasy

A good drop is often imperfect. It may have one or two affixes the player wants, plus flaws or dead stats. The item matters because it has potential: it can be forged, salvaged for shards, or kept as a future crafting base.

## Rarity model

- Normal: base item and implicit only.
- Magic: small affix count, easier to understand, useful early or as a crafting base.
- Rare: larger affix count, usually the main gear chase.
- Unique: build-changing item with fixed identity and special behavior.
- Crafted: allowed for future forge outputs.

## Prefix / suffix logic

Prefixes are major power/stat identity: damage, life, mana, armor, skill scaling.

Suffixes are secondary/utility identity: resistances, speed, cooldown, critical stats, recovery, special utility.

Rare items should respect the readable ARPG shape of up to three prefixes and up to three suffixes.

## Forging potential

Every non-unique item has finite forging potential. Adding, upgrading, removing, or sealing affixes should consume it. Once potential reaches zero, the item is done.

## High-stakes restoration

Late-game restoration content can attempt to restore potential. This should not be a normal crafting button. It should be a risky activity:

- success restores potential;
- partial success restores less;
- failure bricks or destroys the item.

## Uniques

Uniques are build-changing. A unique should create or enable an archetype, not merely add a bigger number. Good unique effects include:

- skill conversion;
- triggered skills;
- resource rule changes;
- support/socket rule changes;
- changing a support skill into a main skill;
- enabling a rare interaction between skill tags.
